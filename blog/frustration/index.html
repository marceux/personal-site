Frustration...

1:41 AM on Sunday Aug 30, 2009

...is part of the competitive experience.

Be it Melee, Chess, Brawl, Go, or even Connect Four, you're going to get mad when your competing against anything and you lose. It's natural.

Even those friendlies you go jigglypuff or ganondorf in, every little loss, regardless of how miniscule, chips away at this fictional brick wall you've built to protect your feelings with. And if the loss is a close one or against someone you think you should have beat, or if you lose because of a silly mistake, that wall is torn down like it was made in Berlin and you're left raw. Angry, sad, or empty, the experience is harrowing. I've seen grown men break chairs, throw hissy-fits, swear uncontrollably, and even cry. We've been there, and we've all done it. It's human.

Now, sure, eventually, you'll meet the Big Bad Wolf that will grab-release forward-smash you because you play Lucas blow down any defense you put up to protect your ego from the vicious spectre of losing; sure you'll be left naked with nothing to stop your ire or your release, whatever it may be, but, and many of us forget this, if it's your first time in a truly competitive environment (and in the case of Brawl/Melee, for many of us it is) you'll learn.

You'll learn to build with brick instead of straw after those first few heart-breaking losses, and then with metal instead of brick, and then finally with rubber instead of metal. We get to the point where we bounce back from losses, or they make their mark and we just go with it, and hopefully learn and reform and not let it tear at us.

We sandbag; we don't enter tournies; we give up, and all in an effort to not get hurt, but think of the growing you're depriving yourself of. Not all of us are blessed with the advantage to succeed from start, and not all of us are strong enough of character to accept losing. That takes real growth, and it'll take quite a bit of growing pains.

But that's ok. If you have a community behind you, and if you're willing to become better as a person and understand what there is to gain, just rip off that band-aid, take the loss, and move forward. A tear or drop of blood or two is not going to kill you. And you know the old cliche. Apparently it'll make you stronger.

tl;dr: Compete to lose; lose to grow; grow to win.
